What is a car title? Everything you need to know.

A car title is a legal document that shows who owns the vehicle and is required for registration or sale. It has vehicle identification number, make and year of purchase, price when vehicle was new, licence plate number, name and address of the registered owner, name of lien holder if vehicle is being financed.

Every time the vehicle's ownership changes or you permanently relocate to a new state, the title must be reissued.

What are clean and salvage titles?

A clean title simply means that the vehicle has never been declared a total loss. It may, however, have mechanical issues, poor repair, and a history of minor to major accidents. Insurance companies will cover the full value of a vehicle with a clean title. You can also use it at the DMV to register your vehicle and obtain new licence plates.

A salvage title is issued when a vehicle is damaged (most likely in an accident) and deemed a total loss by the insurance company. In that case, the vehicle cannot be registered or insured, and its resale value is extremely low.

Selling a car without a title: is it doable?

You cannot legally sell your vehicle unless you have a title. If you do not already have a title, you must apply for one. For more information on obtaining a duplicate title, contact the DMV.

You can also register the vehicle in Vermont because they only require the Bill of Sale and the registration tax. However, the vehicle must be more than 15 years old, and the Bill of Sale must include the buyer and seller's contact information.

How do I calculate my car value?

There are authenticated online tools like VinAudit API that allow you to calculate the value of your car based on functionality, make, model, year, and other vehicle conditions.

Furthermore, you can have your vehicle inspected by a certified dealer, who will explain everything that goes into determining the value of your vehicle in person.

What documents do I need before the sale of my car?

The registration book, warranty book, service history documents, emissions test certificates, bill of sale, owner's manual, warranty documents, inspection sheet are all original and additional documents that your dealer may request.

Selling a Car Privately vs. Selling to a Dealer: What should I do?

It is easier to sell your car to a dealer because the dealer handles all of the paperwork as well as repairing and detailing the vehicle. Though the time spent on these tasks may be deducted from the value of your vehicle, selling it to a dealer is a safe, simple, and quick option. They have analytical tools and professional folks to determine the value of your vehicle.

Selling it to a private buyer, on the other hand, may entail the hassle of gathering all of the necessary paperwork, contacting and arranging a meeting with the buyers, safely transferring the title, and potentially costing you more time and money.
